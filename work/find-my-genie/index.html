01 / Context

A concierge is easy—until it has to operate in the real world.

The product needed to translate a customer’s open-ended request into coordinated outreach across many independent vendors. Availability changes, replies arrive asynchronously, and an AI agent can misunderstand context. The safety principle was straightforward: keep every consequential state traceable, surface uncertainty, and give operators explicit paths to inspect, override, verify, or recover the workflow. That same interaction model is relevant wherever AI operates in regulated or high-stakes environments.

02 / Decisions

Designing for agency,
not magic.

The product made the agent’s work visible enough to build confidence while keeping the customer’s journey calm. The operator experience focused on intervention, context, and recovery rather than simply exposing raw agent logs.

Decision 01

Progress with meaning

Replace vague loading states with clear operational stages users could understand.

Decision 02

Human control

Design intervention, override, and verification patterns as primary product behavior.

Decision 03

Trust across portals

Use one shared status language across customer, vendor, and internal surfaces.

03 / Outcome

From request to booking in a clearer four-step flow.

The redesigned live-status experience reduced the booking journey to four steps and made the parallel vendor workflow legible to both customers and operators. The current draft uses résumé-verified outcomes; interface evidence and deeper iteration history will be added later.
