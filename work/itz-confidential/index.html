01 / The brief

The storefront opens like a case file.

Itz Confidential sells physical mystery games, but the real product begins before the box arrives. The website had to establish atmosphere, explain unfamiliar cases, support confident purchase decisions, and carry the fiction into post-purchase submissions and real-world events.

6Playable mystery cases in the live catalogue
3Connected journeys: discover, purchase, investigate
1Brand language across commerce and live events

02 / Narrative interaction

The brand is not decoration.
It is the navigation model.

Case cards behave like evidence pinned to an investigation board. A continuous red thread turns page movement into a visual relationship between clues, while typewritten summaries, stamps, tape, monochrome imagery, and paper dossiers make browsing feel native to the fictional world.

Decision 01

Enter in character

Visitors are addressed as detectives rather than customers.

Decision 02

Browse evidence

Cases appear as physical files positioned across an investigation board.

Decision 03

Reveal detail

Inspecting a case opens a focused dossier without breaking the scene.

03 / Commerce architecture

Atmosphere earns attention.
Structure earns the decision.

The experience gradually moves from intrigue to comparison. Each case exposes its premise, difficulty, player count, and price before the Suspect Lineup brings individual products and bundle offers into one purchase surface. The fiction stays intact, but the commercial information becomes increasingly explicit.

04 / Beyond purchase

The experience extends beyond the box.

The detective portal gives players a route to verify conclusions and submit an official assessment. A separate events experience translates the product into corporate get-togethers and live mystery formats, extending one identity across direct commerce, play, support, and event sales.

SolvePlayers investigate the physical evidence
SubmitThe detective portal receives their final theory
GatherEvents turn the format into a shared live experience

05 / Design engineering

A cinematic surface still needs
production discipline.

I built the experience as a typed Next.js product with responsive layouts, motion-aware interactions, dynamic loading for heavier commerce surfaces, mobile performance fallbacks, structured product data, generated social previews, and indexable case content.

Application

Next.js + TypeScript

Reusable case data powers both the product interface and structured product metadata.

Interaction

Framer Motion

Intro, dossier transitions, and narrative reveals reinforce the world without blocking core information.

Performance

Adaptive effects

The red-thread and texture system use throttling and mobile fallbacks to protect responsiveness.

Discovery

SEO + social metadata

Sitemaps, robots directives, JSON-LD products, and generated Open Graph imagery support sharing and search.

06 / Outcome

One world across product,
commerce, and community.

The final experience makes the business feel like an investigation from the first impression through case selection, purchase, play, assessment, and events. According to the live brand story, Itz Confidential has delivered more than 5,000 case files and became the top-selling stall at Horror Con.

What I would improve next: bring checkout fully into the branded experience, instrument the case-to-purchase funnel, add structured testimonials near the decision point, and test whether visitors understand the physical product before the atmosphere asks them to explore.
